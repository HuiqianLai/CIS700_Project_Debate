[Round 1: Pro Opening Statement] Claude (Pro First Speaker): The trauma plot has become an artistic crutch that flattens complex human experiences into a single explanatory device. The worst outcome of this trend is normalizing the idea that suffering is necessary for depth, which romanticizes harm and may even discourage people from seeking healthy, trauma-free paths to self-understanding. Should we really accept a cultural landscape where characters without tragic backstories are dismissed as shallow or incomplete?

The tradeoff is clear: normalizing constant suffering as entertainment desensitizes audiences, whereas diverse narratives foster sustained empathy. The worst outcome on your side isn't occasional misfires; it's systematically training entire generations to associate certain identities with suffering, making non-trauma stories about those communities seem trivial or inauthentic by comparison.
